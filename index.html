Fitts's Law Simulator

Fitts's law is used to calculate the expected amount of time that should take for to move to a target. The equation used is t = a + b·log2(d/w)

The sliders below can be moved to adjust the average (mean) amount of time that it would take to accuratly travel from the grey box on the left to the box on the right

  • t = average completion time of movement
  • a = constant determined by input device (acts to add)
  • b = constant determined by input device (acts as a multiplier)
  • d = distance from starting point to center of the target
  • w = width of target (measured along axis of motion)
